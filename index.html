A Worn Analogy “Imagine electricity as the flow of water through a pipeâ€¦" This is a typical, albeit rudimentary, analogy given when learning about concepts of electricity. Its aim is to provides a somewhat intuitive understanding, but largely result in attaching words, such as “voltage” and “current”, to properties of water, such as pressure and speed, with little real correlation. Once explored, the properties of electricity deviate from that of flowing water; to gain a robust understanding, another form of intuitive understanding must be explored.

I’d like to share a short extract from the Book 5 of Meditations by Marcus Aurelius, At day’s first light have in readiness, against disinclination to leave your bed, the thought that ‘I am rising for the work of man’. Must I grumble at setting out to do what I was born for, and for the sake of which I have been brought into the world? Is this the purpose of my creation, to lie here under the blankets and keep myself warm?
